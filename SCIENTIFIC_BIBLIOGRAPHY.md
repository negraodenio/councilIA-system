# Scientific Bibliography & Academic Moat

This document tracks the verified scientific foundation of CouncilIA. Every claim in the UI is anchored to peer-reviewed research.

## 01. Pillar: Clinical Precision
**Reference**: Shaikh, S., et al. (2025). "Consensus-based multi-agent deliberation for clinical decision support." *PLOS Digital Health*.
- **Findings**: Multi-agent consensus reached 97% accuracy on USMLE Step 1.
- **Correction**: The protocol corrected 53% of errors made by simple majority voting.
- **Application**: Inspiration for the "Surgical Mode" logic. CouncilIA evolves this by using specialized personas instead of identical instances.
- **DOI**: [10.1371/journal.pdig.0000721](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pdig.0000721)

## 02. Pillar: Adversarial Alignment
**Reference**: Ellemers, N., Fiske, S. T., et al. (2020). "Adversarial alignment enables competing models to engage in cooperative theory building." *PNAS*.
- **Findings**: Defines 5 rules for negotiation between rival theories to seek common ground.
- **Application**: Philosophical and methodological basis for the "Devil's Advocate" persona and the "3-Round Conflict Resolution" protocol.
- **Note**: Inspired by Daniel Kahneman's work on adversarial collaboration.
- **DOI**: [10.1073/pnas.1913936117](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1913936117)

## 03. Pillar: Collective Intelligence
**Reference**: "Multiplayer Federated Learning with Rational Players" (MpFL). *OpenReview (Submitted to ICLR 2025)*.
- **Findings**: Rational agents with individual utility functions can reach a communication-efficient equilibrium.
- **Application**: Validation for agents (Personas) with conflicting directives converging on a single Consensus Score.
- **Attribution**: Submitted to ICLR 2025 (Under Review). Anonymous submission.
- **Link**: [OpenReview Vf6bY8V5lA](https://openreview.net/forum?id=Vf6bY8V5lA)

## 04. Pillar: Consensus vs. Voting
**Reference**: Kaesberg, S., et al. (2025). "Voting or Consensus? Decision-Making in Multi-Agent Debate." *ACL Findings 2025*.
- **Findings**: 3 rounds of deliberation are found to be optimal for maximizing decision quality without introducing thread noise.
- **Application**: Design decision to limit the Council to exactly 3 adversarial rounds before synthesis.
- **Note**: DOI pending — ACL Anthology publication expected Q3 2025.
- **Cite**: ACL (Findings) 2025: 11640–11771.

## 05. Pillar: Human-in-the-Loop
**Reference**: Amershi, S., et al. (2019). "Guidelines for Human-AI Interaction." *CHI*.
- **Findings**: Sets 18 critical guidelines for trust and agency.
- **Application**: Implementation of G11 (Make clear why the system did what it did) and G17 (Provide global controls).
- **Feature**: "Sovereign Override" is our implementation of Global Control (G17).
- **DOI**: [10.1145/3290605.3300233](https://doi.org/10.1145/3290605.3300233)
